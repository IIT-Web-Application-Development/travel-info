The city of Chicago is the third-most populous city in the United States. After the Great Chicago Fire of 1871, the city concerted effort to rebuild on the damage. By 1900, Chicago became one of the five largest cities in the world! The city is an international hub for finance, commerce, industry, technology, telecommunications, and transfportation: O'Hare iNternational Airport is the second-busiest airport in the world when measured by air traffic.

In 2016, Chicago hosted a record 54 million domestic and international visitors, making it one of the most visited cities in the United State. Landmarks in the city include Millennium Park, Navy Pier, the Magnificent Mile, Art Institute of Chicago, Museum Campus, the Willis (Sears) Tower, Museum of Science and Industry, Lincoln Park Zoo and more. Chicago's culture includes the visual arts, novels, firlm theater (especially improvisational comedy), and jazz, blues, soul, hip-hop, gospel, and house music.
